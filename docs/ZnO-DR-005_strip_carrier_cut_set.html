1 · What makes it the inverse

Same coupon, border, window, groove loop and bolt pattern as the immersion carrier — but flipped and re-materialled for the caustic strip: the solid backer is on the bottom (protecting the high-P face from NaOH), the window is on top (bare-zinc back exposed so H₂ escapes), the seal is EPDM in the backer (FKM fails in caustic), and the clamp is spring-loaded to hold pressure as PP creeps in the hot bath.

2 · Backer plate (½″ PP) — plan

Backer — top face up. Solid PP; EPDM groove and locating pocket on the top (coupon) face; 10 through-holes. This plate protects and seals the high-P face from below.
Clamp frame — ⅜″ PP. Window exposes the bare-zinc back to the bath; frame lands on the 13 mm rim; 10 through-holes + 2 handle-mount holes.

3 · Assembly section & clamp detail

Inverted & spring-clamped. Backer on the bottom protects the high-P face; EPDM O-ring seals its underside; window exposes zinc up; Ti bolts + Belleville springs hold even pressure.
Spring clamp. Belleville washers under each Ti bolt keep the seal loaded as the PP relaxes in the bath; through-bolt + Ti nut (PP threads are weak).
Critical dimension — rim registration. The etch boundary, the seal, and the trim line all live on the 13 mm border, so the coupon locating pocket and the clamp window edge must both land on that line. Hold the window position and the pocket ±0.1 mm; if the clamp lets the etch creep past 13 mm, the etch boundary no longer equals the seal border and the rim/handling frame is compromised.

7 · CAM notes

Groove on backerThe EPDM groove goes on the backer top face; the window is on the clamp frame — verify the two features end up facing the coupon.
Window positionTightest tolerance in the set — it sets the etch/seal line. ±0.1 mm.
Through-boltsNo tapped threads in PP; drill ⌀4.3 through both and use Ti nuts.
PP toolingSharp single-flute, clear chips; PP is gummy but cuts clean at the right feed.

Open item (from DR-001): confirm the unetched 13 mm rim is stiff enough to tension the etched centre through transfer — if not, the front-side support fallback (noted in SOP-050) applies. This is the one assumption in the whole handling chain still to be validated on a real coupon.
