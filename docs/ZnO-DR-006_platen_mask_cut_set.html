1 · Design logic — why the mask is a film, not a ring

During Phase-1 lay-down, the full 100 × 125 rim-framed foil descends onto the plate. Nothing tall can sit over the plate's 13 mm border, or the foil can't land. So the butyl-land mask is a 0.13 mm (0.005″) skived PTFE film — a picture-frame gasket lying on the border, clamped from outside the plate footprint where its overhang drapes onto the platen. Three consequences fall out:

PropertyHow the film delivers it
Land protectionThe film physically covers the plate's 13 mm top-face border for every silicone operation; PTFE releases any stray silicone.
Bondline referenceThe zinc rim lands on the film: rim height = plate + 0.13 mm, so the foil centre sits at a ~0.13 mm bondline — the film doubles as the bondline stop (~100 µm target).
Underside landThe plate's bottom-face butyl land is masked by contact with the platen itself — no second mask needed.

2 · The fixture stack, in section

How it works. Vacuum holds the plate; the film covers the 13 mm land and overhangs onto the platen; the frame clamps the overhang; the rim lands on the film, which sets the bondline. Underside land is masked by the platen face.
Film dimensions. Outer 130 × 155, window 74 × 99, both centred — a 13 mm land cover plus 15 mm overhang per side. Hand-cut with knife + straightedge (all rectangles), or die-cut for volume. Replace when contaminated.

3 · Platen plan (½″ MIC-6 aluminium, 200 × 230)

Platen — top face. Shallow locating pocket for the plate; vacuum loop inset 8 mm inside the plate footprint (6.5 mm bare seal land to the plate edge) plus a cross; centre port ⌀5 back-drilled to a fitting; corner dowels and mid-side M5 taps for the frame.
Clamp frame — ¼″ PVDF. Registers on the platen dowels; nylon/PVDF M5 thumbscrews press the film overhang against the platen around the plate. One frame serves both plate thicknesses — it never sits over the plate.
Vacuum seal land. The groove loop's outer edge sits 6.5 mm inside the plate edge, leaving a continuous bare aluminium land under the plate for the vacuum seal. Both cell-plate thicknesses share the 100×125 footprint, so one groove pattern serves the whole line. The end plate's ribbed outer face goes up (ribs never touch the platen); its flat inner face is the vacuum side.

6 · Machining & use notes

ItemNote
Surface LAST, in placeFace the platen top flat as the final op, on the router that will use it — flatness in the machine's own frame is the whole point of the fixture. Skim ≥0.1 mm after grooves/holes to remove burr lips.
Order of opsProfile → pocket → grooves → drill/tap → back c'bore → facing pass last → deburr groove edges lightly (no rounding of the seal land).
Aluminium cuttingSingle-flute carbide + mist lube per DR-004 practice; ½″ MIC-6 in multiple depth passes.
Film disciplineThe film is the contamination barrier — treat as consumable. Replace whenever silicone is seen on it; never flip a used film. Store films flat and clean.
Leak checkWith a plate loaded, gauge should hold near-full vacuum with the venturi valved off for ≥1 min. If not: check pocket burrs and land cleanliness before suspecting the plate.
Gate linkagePassing SOP-050 gate G4 (butyl lands silicone-free) presumes the film was intact and correctly clamped for every silicone operation — log film changes with run IDs.
