1 · One design, three units — now round

The coupon is clamped between a solid back / mask plate and a window plate: the window frames the ⌀130 active area, the FKM O-ring seals the 13 mm annular border, and only the working face (through the window) meets the bath. The same two routed plates serve all three carriers; what changes per unit is the material margin, the fasteners, and how it is used — never the geometry, so one DXF cuts every unit.

Rev 2.0 — round wafer-scale coupon. The rectangular 100 × 125 coupon is replaced by a ⌀156 round zinc blank (0.15 mm foil) yielding a ⌀130 etched active circle to back a 150 mm silicon wafer (ZnO-SOP-050 Rev 4.0). Every rectangle in the old set becomes a circle; the bolt rectangle becomes a ⌀174 bolt circle.

2 · Window plate & back plate (¼″ PVDF) — plans

Window plate — coupon face up. The ⌀130 window frames the active area; the FKM groove is a ⌀143 circle ringing it on the coupon face, 6.5 mm outside the window edge; 12 M4 clearance holes on the ⌀174 bolt circle.
Back / mask plate — coupon face up. Solid PVDF; 0.15 mm ⌀156.3 pocket registers the round coupon; 12 holes on the shared ⌀174 circle, drilled ⌀3.3 and tapped M4 (or opened to ⌀4.3 and through-bolted); 2 handle-mount taps at the top.

3 · Handle strip & assembly section

Handle. Routed 200 × 25 strip from the ¼″ PVDF nest, bolted with 2× M4 into the back-plate taps. A ½″ PVDF rod threaded into one tap is the bought alternative (DR-002 §3a).
Face-down, tilted, sealed. The back plate masks the reverse face, the ⌀143 O-ring seals the border, and the ⌀130 window opens the working face downward so homogeneous particles fall clear; unequal PTFE legs give the ~5° tilt of DR-001/DR-002.
Critical dimension — border registration. The window edge (⌀130) and the locating pocket (⌀156.3) together set the 13 mm masked border on the ⌀156 coupon, and the O-ring centreline (⌀143) bisects that border — 6.5 mm to the coupon edge, 6.5 mm to the window edge. Hold the window diameter and the pocket concentric to the bolt circle within ±0.1 mm: this is the same border that later becomes the strip clamp line and the zinc handling rim (SP-001, "one border").
Round-O-ring bonus. The seal groove is now a plain circle, so the old "splice the cord on a straight run, never at a corner" rule is gone — there are no corners. Splice anywhere on the ring; a circular gland also loads the cord evenly all the way round.

4 · Hole pattern — datum bottom-left (0,0), both plates

Twelve holes on a ⌀174 bolt circle centred on the plate (part-local centre 95, 95), first hole at 15° then every 30°. The pattern is shared with the strip carrier (DR-005) — one drilling template serves the whole fixture set. Sizes differ by plate: window plate ⌀4.3 (M4 clearance); back plate ⌀3.3, tapped M4 after routing (or opened to ⌀4.3 and through-bolted + nut per DR-002 §7). Handle-mount (back plate only): 2× ⌀3.3 tap M4 at (80, 169) and (110, 169). Handle strip: 2× ⌀4.3 at (10, 12.5) and (40, 12.5).

7 · CAM & build notes

ItemNote
Source fileThe round ⌀190 plate is larger than half the 300 × 287 bed, so it nests one plate per bed: the ¼″ PVDF file lays the window plate + handle on bed A and the back plate on bed B — see ZnO-DR-004.
Window diameterTightest tolerance in the part — it sets the active-area / border line. ±0.1 mm, concentric to the bolt circle.
Back-plate holesThe DXF drills the back plate at ⌀3.3 on purpose: tap M4 by hand after routing. If through-bolting instead, open to ⌀4.3 before assembly.
GrooveCircular ⌀143 gland — no corner fillet needed. Route to 1.1 mm depth, 2.1 mm wide.
Order of opsGroove/pocket → drill → window → outer profile last (tabs or vacuum hold-down).
DeburrDeburr all edges — the O-ring must seat and the 0.15 mm zinc-backed foil must not be nicked at the pocket or window.
AssemblySplice and seat the O-ring; drop the coupon into the pocket, working face toward the window; land the window plate; torque the twelve bolts evenly in a star pattern until the cord takes even squeeze.
Unit disciplineLabel each built unit (GROWTH / PLATING / SEEDING) and never swap: the seeded fixture must stay out of nickel, and nickel- or caustic-fouled fixtures never return upstream (DR-001 §2).
