1 · Governing principles (read first)

Two-phase adhesive rule. All silicone bonds are made and cured in Phase 1 with the butyl lands physically masked by the bonding carrier. All butyl joints are made in Phase 2 by compression as each plate lands. Silicone and butyl never coexist wet, and no silicone ever touches a butyl land or an enclosed inner face.
Butyl-by-compression. Every inner interface is uncured PIB squeezed to contact against the spacer stop — sealed on both faces the instant the plate lands. Closing a cavity and finishing its seal are the same act. The seal is good for decades only while compressed: the outboard structure exists to hold that squeeze permanently.
One border. The 13 mm border is the strip clamp, then the zinc-rim handling frame, then (after trim) the butyl seal land on clean aluminium. Etch boundary = trim line = seal land. Hold it to ±0.1 mm wherever it is registered.
Working face discipline. The mid-P void surface is never touched, never pressed, never sees silicone; face-down in wet baths, face-up in dry steps; foil is laid onto wetted plates, never pressed under them.
