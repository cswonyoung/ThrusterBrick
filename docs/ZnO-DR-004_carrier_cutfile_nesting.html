1 · Packing result

The 140 × 166 plate is large relative to a 300 × 287 bed: two fit (side by side, 280 mm across), and no orientation admits a third — a third plate needs a 140 mm minimum dimension and only a 121 mm strip remains. So the ceiling is two plates per sheet, and the nests below use the leftover top strip for the handle strip. Utilisation runs ~54–60 %; the balance is inherent to this plate-to-bed ratio, not the packing.

2 · The three cut files

¼″ PVDFZnO_cut_PVDF_quarter.dxf
Window + back plate + handle strip — one full sandwich carrier per sheet (immersion or seeding). sheet utilisation 59.8%
½″ PPZnO_cut_PP_half.dxf
Two strip-carrier backer plates — two carriers per sheet. sheet utilisation 54.0%
⅜″ PPZnO_cut_PP_threeEighth.dxf
Two strip-carrier clamp frames — two carriers per sheet. sheet utilisation 54.0%

Layers in each DXF: CUT (outline) WINDOW GROOVE (O-ring centreline) POCKET · DRILL (holes) · SHEET (bed outline).

Worked example — one full line (2 immersion + 1 seeding + 1 strip): 3 × ¼″ PVDF sheets, 1 × ½″ PP sheet (holds 2 backers — one spare), 1 × ⅜″ PP sheet (2 frames — one spare). For M lines: 3M PVDF, ⌈M/2⌉ each of ½″ and ⅜″ PP. Strip parts pair up two-per-sheet, so build strip carriers in even batches to avoid a half-used PP sheet.

5 · CAM notes for the DXF

ItemNote
Units / datumMillimetres, origin at the sheet bottom-left (0,0); load with Y-up.
Layer → operationCUT/WINDOW = profile through; DRILL = drill/tap; GROOVE = pocket to 1.1 mm depth; POCKET = 0.15 mm locating recess.
Groove cornersDXF groove is drawn square — apply R ≥ 5 mm fillets in CAM so the O-ring cord bends without kinking.
Hole sizes⌀4.3 = M4 clearance; ⌀3.3 = M4 tap drill (tap after).
Order of opsPocket/groove → drill → windows → outer profile last (tabs or vacuum hold-down keep parts anchored).
Hold-downSheet fills the bed — use vacuum or perimeter tape; there is no waste frame to clamp.
PP toolingSharp single-flute; clear chips; PP is gummy but cuts clean at the right feed.
