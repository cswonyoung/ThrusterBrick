1 · Design logic — hold a wafer flat, protect its polished face

Phase 1 now die-attaches the Ni-P nanovoid foil (high-P face) to the unpolished back of a 150 mm wafer with a low-outgassing silicone (ZnO-SOP-050 Rev 4.0). The chuck must do three things:

RequirementHow the chuck delivers it
Hold the wafer dead flat through cureShallow ⌀150.5 locating recess + concentric vacuum grooves pull the wafer to a faced-flat top.
Wafer polished-face downBonding is on the up-facing rough side; the polished side rests on the chuck and must be preserved (it is the neighbour's void cap in SOP-060).
Never mark the polished faceA clean, compliant, silicone-free chuck pad lines the recess so the polished silicon never touches bare aluminium; vacuum reaches the wafer through the pad.

2 · Chuck plan & section

Chuck — top face. ⌀150.5 wafer recess (0.2 dp); two vacuum rings (⌀120, ⌀60) linked by a cross to the centre ⌀5 port (back-drilled to a fitting); four M5 foot taps at ⌀170. Surface the top face flat as the final op.
How it works. Vacuum through the pad holds the wafer polished-face-down and dead flat; silicone is metered on the up-facing rough side; the rim-framed foil is laid on high-P-down, void-up, never pressed (ZnO-SOP-050 §6.3). Cure near-RT to limit CTE bow.

5 · Machining & use notes

ItemNote
Surface LAST, in placeFace the chuck top flat as the final op, on the router that will use it — flatness in the machine's own frame is the point. Skim ≥0.1 mm after grooves/recess to remove burr lips.
Order of opsProfile ⌀190 → recess ⌀150.5 × 0.2 → vacuum rings + cross → drill/tap (port, feet) → back c'bore → facing pass last → deburr groove edges lightly.
Pad disciplineThe pad is the only thing between the polished wafer face and aluminium — keep it clean, particle-free, and silicone-free (a stray silicone film here would transfer to the polished cap). Replace when marked.
Polished-face gateThe wafer's polished face is inspected at release (ZnO-SOP-050 §6.4). Any scratch or particle transfer from a dirty pad fails the gate — treat the pad as a cleanliness-critical consumable.
Leak checkWith a wafer + pad loaded, the gauge should hold near-full vacuum with the venturi valved off for ≥1 min. If not: check recess/groove burrs and pad seating.
No mask, no frameThere is no film mask and no clamp frame in this revision. Ignore any reference to ZnO_cut_frame_PVDF.dxf — that file is retired.
