Derived from the 150 mm ZnO-DR-005 Rev 2.0 by scaling the carrier and coupon geometry to the 300 mm flight line; the caustic-strip chemistry, materials, seal cord, and plate thicknesses are unchanged — only diameters, the bolt count, and part-local coordinates scale. 1 · What makes it the inverse

Same coupon, border, window, groove ring and bolt circle as the immersion carrier — but flipped and re-materialled for the caustic strip: the solid backer is on the bottom (protecting the high-P face from NaOH), the window is on top (bare-zinc back exposed so H₂ escapes), the seal is EPDM in the backer (FKM fails in caustic), and the clamp is spring-loaded to hold pressure as PP creeps in the hot bath.

Round wafer-scale coupon (300 mm). The 0.15 mm zinc blank is ⌀306 with a ⌀280 etched window and a 13 mm handling rim; every feature is concentric on the shared ⌀330 bolt circle. The 0.15 mm zinc gives a 13 mm rim markedly stiffer than the old 25 µm stock — good for tensioning the larger etched centre through transfer.

2 · Backer plate (½″ PP) & clamp frame (⅜″ PP) — plans

To-scale vs. schematic. The to-scale plans above are generated from the datum/DXF geometry and are authoritative. The hand drawings below are schematic (not to scale), kept for annotation and assembly context.
Backer — top face up. Solid PP; EPDM ⌀296 groove and ⌀306.3 pocket on the top (coupon) face; 16 through-holes. This plate protects and seals the high-P face from below.
Clamp frame — ⅜″ PP. ⌀280 window exposes the bare-zinc back to the bath; frame lands on the 13 mm rim; 16 through-holes + 2 handle-mount holes. All through (PP threads are weak).

3 · Assembly section & clamp detail

Inverted & spring-clamped. Backer on the bottom protects the high-P face; EPDM ⌀296 O-ring seals its underside; window exposes zinc up; Ti bolts + Belleville springs hold even pressure.
Spring clamp. Belleville washers under each Ti bolt keep the seal loaded as the PP relaxes in the bath; through-bolt + Ti nut (PP threads are weak).
Critical dimension — rim registration. The etch boundary, the seal, and the trim line all live on the 13 mm border, so the coupon locating pocket (⌀306.3) and the clamp window edge (⌀280) must both land on that line, concentric to the ⌀330 bolt circle. Hold the window diameter and the pocket ±0.1 mm; if the clamp lets the etch creep past 13 mm, the etch boundary no longer equals the seal border and the rim/handling frame is compromised.
Round-O-ring bonus. The EPDM gland is a plain ⌀296 circle — no corner splice to leak in hot NaOH, and even squeeze all round.

4 · Hole pattern — datum bottom-left (0,0)

Sixteen holes on a ⌀330 bolt circle centred on the plate (part-local centre 175, 175), first hole at 11.25° then every 22.5° — the same coordinates as DR-003-300 §4. All are ⌀4.3 through both plates (M4 Ti bolt + Belleville + nut). Handle-mount (frame only): 2× ⌀4.3 at (147, 311) and (203, 311). No tapping — PP threads are weak, so through-bolt everything.
