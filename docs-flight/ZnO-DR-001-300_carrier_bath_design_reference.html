1 · Purpose

This reference fixes the handling, foil orientation, carrier construction, and bath layout across the 300 mm process line. It captures three principles that emerged during design: dedicated fixtures per chemistry (so no seeded, silver-bearing carrier ever enters a nickel bath, and no nickel- or caustic-fouled carrier returns to a clean step); the working face is gripped only by edges and the intact zinc backbone, never touched directly; and baths are single-use, so vessels are rectangular and volume-minimised to cut reagent and waste.

Amber marks the working face (the mid-P void surface) in every drawing, so orientation is readable at a glance.

2 · Handling principles

PrincipleConsequence
Isolate chemistriesA dedicated carrier per chemistry; the key switch is seeding → plating (keep nickel out of the Ag-seeded fixture).
Never touch the working faceGrip edges and, until the strip, the rigid zinc backbone; after the strip, grip the stiff zinc rim.
Orientation follows the stepFace-down / tilted in every immersion/deposition bath (particles fall away); face-up for dry line-of-sight steps and the final lay-down.
Single-use, minimal volumeRectangular vessels sized to the coupon + carrier + ~10–15 mm clearance; ~2–3 L per charge on a stir-hotplate (~4× the 150 mm charge).
Grab-and-go handleEach immersion carrier carries a handle that stays above the liquid; it doubles as a drain/hang point.

6 · Material cautions

RuleWhy
Gaskets: FKM/Viton or PTFE — never siliconea silicone gasket sheds contamination onto the working face and later outgasses inside the sealed common-vacuum stack (ZnO-SOP-060-300)
Plating carrier: non-catalytic plastic, silver-freeelectroless Ni plates only the foil, not the fixture; no stray silver drag-in
Strip carrier: no aluminium, no glassboth are attacked by hot NaOH (Al dissolves; glass slowly etches)
Seeding carrier: no bare metalreactive metal would reduce/consume the silver bath

8 · Summary

Three carrier designs cover the line — an immersion process carrier (built as two dedicated units for growth and plating), a seeding carrier, and a strip edge-clamp carrier — plus a flat wafer bonding chuck that is not a carrier. At 300 mm every fixture frames the ⌀306 coupon / ⌀280 active region, and all baths are single-use, volume-minimised (~4–6 L) on stir-hotplates; growth runs static, plating and strip run stirred via a bar beneath the standoff. The working face is amber in every drawing and is never contacted directly: face-down in the wet baths, face-up for the dry and die-attach steps.

Strip orientationConfirm face-up strip (H₂ escape) vs the backing/solution-access trade for your carrier.
