1 · Packing result — one plate per bed

The round ⌀350 carrier plate is larger than half the 500 mm bed width, so only one plate fits per sheet — two ⌀350 circles cannot be placed on a 500 × 600 bed at any angle (their centres would need to be ≥350 mm apart, but the usable inner box only allows ~250 mm along the long axis once workholding margin is removed). This is set by the ⌀306 wafer-scale coupon plus the bolt circle that must ring it, and is inherent to the plate-to-bed ratio, not the packing. Utilisation runs ~32–38 %; the balance is unavoidable at this size. (A ⌀306 blank alone would nest two-up diagonally on the 500 × 600 sheet, but the part cut here is the ⌀350 plate.)

Worked example — one full line (2 immersion + 1 seeding + 1 strip): 6 × ¼″ PVDF sheets (3 sandwich carriers × 2 beds each), 1 × ½″ PP (1 backer), 1 × ⅜″ PP (1 frame). For M lines: 6M PVDF, M each of ½″ and ⅜″ PP. Since each round plate is one bed, sheet count scales linearly — batch cutting saves setup time, not sheets. At 300 mm the sheets are larger (500 × 600) and one plate each, so the per-line sheet count is unchanged from the 150 mm line; only sheet size and material cost per sheet rise.

5 · CAM notes for the DXF

ItemNote
Units / datumMillimetres, origin at the sheet bottom-left (0,0); load with Y-up. The PVDF file carries both beds side by side (bed B offset +530 in X on the 500 mm-wide sheet).
Layer → operationCUT/WINDOW = profile through; DRILL = drill/tap; GROOVE = pocket to 1.1 mm depth; POCKET = 0.15 mm locating recess.
GrooveCircular ⌀296 gland — no corner fillets to add. Route 2.1 wide × 1.1 deep (land widths held constant from the 150 mm line).
Hole sizes⌀4.3 = M4 clearance; ⌀3.3 = M4 tap drill (tap after, PVDF back plate only). Bolt pattern is ⌀330 ×16 M4.
Order of opsPocket/groove → drill → windows → outer profile last (tabs or vacuum hold-down).
PP toolingSharp single-flute; clear chips; PP is gummy but cuts clean at the right feed. Larger ⌀350 profiles want more tabs / stronger hold-down than at 150 mm.
