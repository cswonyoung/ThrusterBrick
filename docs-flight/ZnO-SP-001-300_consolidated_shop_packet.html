Rev 1.0 — 300 mm scale-up (derived from the 150 mm ZnO-SP-001 Rev 2.0). This packet indexes the 300 mm flying line (docs-flight/), obtained by scaling the wafer ⌀150 → ⌀300. What scaled: wafer ⌀300 × 0.775 mm (active ⌀280), zinc blank ⌀306, Ni-P 25 µm, per-cell pitch ~0.875 mm / mass ~145 g; carrier ⌀350 (window ⌀280 · O-ring ⌀296 · pocket ⌀306.3 · bolt circle ⌀330 ×16 M4) on a 500 × 600 mm bed; bath / reagent volumes ~4× (growth 4–6 L · electroless Ni-P 8–12 L · caustic strip 4–6 L; seeding vessels ~⌀340–360 mm); getter ~4× area; chamber sealer clears ⌀300+. What is unchanged: the chemistry, the working-face discipline, the low-outgassing-silicone + getter rules, and the shiny-Si-down orientation. All dimensions cite 300mm-datum-reference.md — the single source of truth; they are not re-derived here. The ZnO_cut_frame_PVDF cut file is retired. Release-pass items (manifest, checksums, README, filename normalization, mirrors, DXF generation) are pending.
Rev 1.1 — ZnO seed layer at op 10. SOP-010-300 (Rev 1.1, tracking the 150 mm SOP-010 Rev 3.0) adds a ZnO seed layer between the acetic light etch and growth: 5 mM zinc acetate dihydrate in absolute ethanol, 3–5 flood coats (~10–15 mL each at ⌀306, ethanol rinse each), annealed 300 ± 25 °C / 20–30 min in air on a ≥ ⌀320 flat plate — oven preferred at this size for edge-to-edge temperature. Replaces bare-foil self-seeding so the array grows c-axis vertical with a tight size/tilt distribution, centre-to-edge. No acid touches the foil after seeding (ZnO dissolves in weak acids). Naming discipline: "ZnO seed layer" (op 10) vs "silver seeding" (op 20) — different steps, never interchangeable.

1 · Governing principles (read first)

Vacuum, not fill. The finished stack shares one common vacuum inside a single hermetic metallized-barrier envelope, held for life by a getter. The seal carries a permanent ~1 atm (~101 kPa) differential, so the hermetic duty lives entirely in the outer envelope — no inner interface is a pressure boundary. Achievable vacuum is rough–medium (~1–100 Pa), not UHV.
Contact, not glue, at the working face. Each foil's mid-P void face rests directly on the neighbour's polished silicon — mechanical contact only, held in compression by atmospheric pressure through the conformal envelope. No adhesive touches the working face (nothing wicks into the voids; the polished-Si cap geometry is preserved).
Low-outgassing inside the vacuum. The only adhesive in the build is the low-outgassing (ASTM E595: TML<1.0 % / CVCM<0.1 %) silicone die-attaching the Ni high-P face to the wafer's unpolished back. A general O₂/H₂O + molecular-sieve (±NEG) getter pumps the permeation + outgassing load for the service life. (This inverts the old N₂-sparing getter rule.)
One border · working-face discipline. The 13 mm border is the strip clamp, then the zinc handling rim, then trimmed off after the wafer bond (there is no butyl seal land now). The mid-P void surface is never touched, never pressed, never sees silicone; face-down in wet baths, face-up in dry steps and die-attach; finally it rests on polished Si under vacuum. Hold the border to ±0.1 mm wherever it is registered.
